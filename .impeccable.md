## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are non-technical users. They arrive on the homepage to quickly understand what the tool does and decide whether to try it. They use the dashboard to generate content and manage the results without feeling overwhelmed by system details.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel calm, high-end, and approachable. The tone should be clear rather than clever, restrained rather than flashy, and confident without looking technical or enterprise-heavy.

### Aesthetic Direction
The target direction is a light, minimalist interface inspired by the emotional restraint of Apple and Notion. The visual system should use a neutral palette, soft contrast, generous whitespace, quiet surfaces, and strong typographic hierarchy. The interface should avoid bright accents, noisy decoration, crowded dashboards, and anything that feels overly SaaS-marketing-driven.

### Design Principles
1. Clarity before decoration: every surface should help non-technical users understand what to do next.
2. Quiet hierarchy: use whitespace, alignment, and typography as the main tools for emphasis.
3. Restrained color: keep the palette neutral and let functional states carry the only notable color shifts.
4. Fast orientation: the homepage should explain value quickly, and the dashboard should surface the primary task immediately.
5. Premium simplicity: reduce friction and visual noise so the product feels calm, polished, and trustworthy.
